# IR Portugal Code of Conduct

IR Portugal aims to support serious research collaboration across different backgrounds, institutions and levels of experience. This requires respectful, safe and intellectually honest working spaces.

This code applies to project repositories, reviews, discussions, meetings and other spaces operated by IR Portugal.

## Shared commitments

Communicate respectfully and constructively. Critique ideas, methods and evidence without personal attacks, intimidation or dismissive behaviour.

Harassment, discrimination and exclusionary conduct are not acceptable.

Approach technical disagreement in good faith. State assumptions, provide evidence, acknowledge uncertainty and allow people to revise or defend their reasoning.

Attribute ideas, code, data and labour accurately. Agree authorship and contributor recognition early and revisit it when responsibilities change.

Use data responsibly. Respect licences, consent, privacy and security, and do not publish sensitive data without appropriate safeguards.

Respect confidentiality where it has been explicitly agreed. IR Portugal works openly by default, so clarify constraints before information is shared.

Disclose financial, professional or personal interests that could reasonably affect project decisions, reviews or publication.

## Reporting concerns

A private reporting address is being established during the founding process. Until it is configured, do not post sensitive reports in a public issue. Contact an identified project lead privately, or pause participation until a safe route is available.

Responses should be proportionate, documented and focused on safety and constructive participation. IR Portugal does not currently claim to have a formal disciplinary committee.

This is an initial standard and should be reviewed publicly as the community develops.
